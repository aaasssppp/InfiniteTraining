Chocolates

Types of Chocolates

Dark Chocolate
Chocolate that contains a high percentage of cocoa solids and cocoa butter, with little or no milk solids. It has a rich, intense flavor.
Milk Chocolate
A sweeter and creamier chocolate made with cocoa, sugar, and milk powder or condensed milk.
White Chocolate
Technically not true chocolate since it contains no cocoa solids—only cocoa butter, sugar, and milk. It's sweet and smooth.
Ruby Chocolate
A naturally pink chocolate made from ruby cocoa beans, with a fruity and slightly tangy flavor.
Compound Chocolate
A budget-friendly chocolate substitute made with cocoa powder and vegetable fats instead of cocoa butter. Often used in baking and coating.
